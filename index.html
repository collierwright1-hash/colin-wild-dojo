How the program works

An Animal Flow practice built for an 11-year-old climber, themed as a martial-arts "breathing styles" game. Five styles, each a real Animal Flow movement family. The first two Forms of every style are open from day one (so there's always variety); the harder Forms 3–5 unlock as he masters the one before. Mastering a Form takes a set number of separate days of practice — tapping "I practiced this" counts once per day, which rewards showing up consistently and keeps daily volume light on the joints. Mastered forms chain into Flows.

  • Every day (~5 min): Total Focus ritual — wrist prep, a few slow breaths, and the week's featured Form. Builds the streak.
  • Tue & Sat (~15–20 min): a full Training Session across 2–3 styles plus a flow and a grip finisher.
  • Thu: climbing day — the dojo just nudges a few focus breaths at the wall.

Joint-stability first (hypermobility note)

Every cue favors active, stacked control over end-range. Throughout you'll see "soft elbows / don't lock," "stack your joints," and "press the floor away — don't sink." Holds are closed-chain activations (Beast, Crab) that teach the joints to stabilize rather than hang on ligaments. Hangs are active (shoulders engaged), never passive dead hangs. Deep passive stretching is intentionally left out.
